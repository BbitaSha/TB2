My Thoughts

There's so much to say about "From Up on Poppy Hill". Maybe I should start with the fact that there was one time where I watched it 3 days in a row. There's just something so special about this story that takes place in 1960's Yokohama, Japan. There's also a clear theme with all of these movies. They're all coming of age stories. You got me. I'm guilty. Take me away, officer!

Look at this scene! Goddamn brilliant!

This is one of the two romantic love stories on this list. I'm not a big fan of romance, but this movie, as cliche as it might be, just does it in such a heart warming way. Even my cold, crule soul turns a lighter shade of grey when I watch this. I love how the school kids are exactly that: school kids. They're caricatures of grown-ups, and it's so cute to watch.

This is one of my favorite parts of the movie - the club house. It's so detailed. Whenever I watch this movie I wish I could go and explore each room of the building. Once again, the movie presents a small, normal life to the audience, but it's so satisfying to watch. Please watch this one in the only way that is right - by watching it 3 days in a row. You're welcome.
